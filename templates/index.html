Optimization Methods

∂

Gradient Descent

First-order iterative method. Updates weights proportional to the negative gradient of the loss surface. Convergence depends on learning rate α.

w ← w − α · ∇L(w)
  • ✓ Scalable to large datasets
  • ✓ Tunable learning rate
  • ✗ Slower convergence
  • ✗ Sensitive to α choice

H

Newton's Method

Second-order method using the Hessian matrix. Achieves quadratic convergence by incorporating curvature information — typically converges in <20 iterations.

w ← w − H⁻¹ · ∇L(w)
  • ✓ Quadratic convergence
  • ✓ No learning rate to tune
  • ✗ O(d²) Hessian cost
  • ✗ Requires invertible H
